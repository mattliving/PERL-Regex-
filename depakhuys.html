De Pakhuys

From South Africa Rock Climbing Routes Wiki

Jump to: navigation, search

The bouldering and sport climbing are on Thys Kruger's farm which is 26km from Clanwilliam, just over the Pakhuis Pass.

Access Issues

This land is privately-owned. The area will be closed if the following rules are disobeyed:

  • Do not enter without paying the required entry fees.
  • Do not litter (e.g. cigarette butts, finger tape).
  • Do not damage or remove vegetation.
  • Do not wander off existing paths.
  • Do not feed wild animals.
  • Do not bolt without the landowner's permission.
  • Bury any toilet paper etc. in a deep hole (>10cm).

Accommodation

De Pakhuys (phone 027 482 1468) offers camping and cottages. It is 26km from Clanwilliam, just over the Pakhuis Pass (second turnoff to the right after the pass). Camping costs R40 per person per night (June 2008).

Sport Routes

The sport routes are on large boulders at the bouldering area called Fields of Joy. From the campsite, drive a few hundred meters back towards the farm house, then take a left turn at the gate and drive for a few hundred metres more. Park and walk up the steepish hillside to the left, following the stone cairns to the boulders. The walk-in takes about 10 minutes.

Ask permission of the landowners Thys and Mark before bolting any new line.

Fields of Joy

A field of quite big boulders, under a cliff line, laying gently on a platform, just above the cliffs down to the valley. The sandstone almost looks like rough granite, the lines are often big and obvious. Friction is very good. It covers many styles of climbing, from the highball slab to roof climbing. Boulders facing to all kind of directions.

Plateau Boulders

On the far left side of the whole area, where the cliff lines fade out and a kind of plateau forms, just before everything drops down to the gorge, there are some fine boulders to be found. The boulders are not that big, the landing is very good and each boulders has a handful of problems.
